Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission)

Description

Launched on 2nd October 2014, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan aims to achieve universal sanitation and cleanliness across India by 2nd October 2019, coinciding with Mahatma Gandhi's 150th birth anniversary

Key Features:

Swachh Bharat Abhiyan focuses on the construction of toilets, solid waste management, and behavioral change towards cleanliness. The mission aims to eliminate open defecation, promote toilet usage, and ensure the proper disposal of waste through segregation and recycling.

Various components of the mission include the construction of individual household toilets, community toilets, public awareness campaigns, and capacity building at the grassroots level.

Swachh Bharat Abhiyan involves the active participation of central and state governments, urban local bodies, NGOs, corporate sector, and citizens to achieve its goals.

Implementation: The mission is implemented through a collaborative effort involving government agencies, local authorities, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and community participation. Financial assistance and technical support are provided to states and local bodies for the construction of toilets and infrastructure development.
