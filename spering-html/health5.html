Description

he Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana (PMBJP) is an initiative of the Government of India launched in 2008 with the aim of providing quality generic medicines at affordable prices to the masses. Under this scheme, Jan Aushadhi Kendras (JAKs) are established across the country to sell generic medicines, which are equivalent in quality and efficacy to branded medicines but are available at a significantly lower cost.

How To Apply For This Scheme

Eligibility: Individuals, pharmacists, doctors, registered medical practitioners, NGOs, charitable organizations, or institutions can apply for opening a Jan Aushadhi Kendra.

Application Process: Interested applicants can visit the official PMBJP website or contact the Bureau of Pharma PSUs of India (BPPI) to obtain the application form for opening a Jan Aushadhi Kendra.

Submission of Application: The application form, along with the required documents and fees, needs to be submitted to the designated authority, which is typically the State Drug Controller or the District Health Department.

Site Inspection and Approval: Upon receipt of the application, the authorities conduct a site inspection to assess the suitability of the proposed location for establishing the Jan Aushadhi Kendra. If the site meets the necessary criteria and requirements, approval is granted for setting up the Kendra.

Procurement of Medicines and Setup: Once approval is obtained, the Kendra operator can procure generic medicines from authorized suppliers and set up the Kendra as per the guidelines provided by BPPI.

Operation and Management: The Jan Aushadhi Kendra operates as a retail pharmacy outlet, providing generic medicines to customers at affordable prices. The Kendra operator is responsible for managing the day-to-day operations, maintaining inventory, ensuring compliance with quality standards, and providing quality service to customers.

Monitoring and Support: BPPI provides support and guidance to Jan Aushadhi Kendra operators, including training programs, marketing support, and assistance in procurement and supply chain management. Regular monitoring and evaluation are conducted to ensure the effective implementation of the scheme and the availability of quality medicines to the public at large.
