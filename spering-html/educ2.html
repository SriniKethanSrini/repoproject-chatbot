Description

The Rashtriya Avishkar Abhiyan (RAA) is a Government of India initiative designed to cultivate a spirit of innovation and scientific inquiry among school students from classes 6 to 12. Through hands-on learning experiences, teacher training programs, and collaborations with various stakeholders, RAA aims to foster creativity, problem-solving skills, and an interest in science, mathematics, and technology subjects. By providing infrastructure support, organizing competitions, and promoting experiential learning, RAA seeks to nurture the next generation of innovators and thinkers who can contribute to India's development and progress.

How To Apply Participate

To participate in the Rashtriya Avishkar Abhiyan (RAA) scheme, schools and educational institutions typically need to follow certain guidelines and procedures set by the government. Here's a general outline of how schools can engage with the RAA initiative:

1.)Awareness and Registration: Schools should first become aware of the RAA initiative and its objectives. They can visit the official website of the Ministry of Education or relevant educational authorities to learn about the scheme's details and registration process.

2.)Contacting Authorities: Schools may need to reach out to the education department or district-level authorities responsible for implementing the RAA program. They can inquire about the application process and any specific requirements for participation.

3.)Application Submission: Once schools have gathered the necessary information and documents, they can submit an application to the designated authorities. This application may include details such as the school's name, address, contact information, number of students, and proposed activities or projects for participation in RAA.

4.) Compliance and Approval: The submitted applications are reviewed by the relevant authorities to ensure compliance with RAA guidelines and objectives. If the application meets the criteria, the school may receive approval to participate in the program.

5.)Implementation: Upon approval, schools can start implementing RAA activities and projects as per the guidelines provided by the authorities. This may involve organizing experiential learning sessions, conducting teacher training programs, setting up innovation labs, and participating in competitions and exhibitions.

6.)Monitoring and Evaluation: Schools are expected to actively engage in RAA activities and regularly report their progress to the authorities. Monitoring and evaluation mechanisms may be in place to assess the impact of the program and ensure its effectiveness.
