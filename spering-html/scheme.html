Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

Description

The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a financial inclusion initiative launched by the Government of India on 28th August 2014. It is one of the world's largest financial inclusion initiatives aimed at providing access to various financial services to the economically weaker sections of society, particularly those who do not have access to banking services.

Key features and objectives of PMJDY include:

Universal Access to Banking: PMJDY aims to ensure that every household in India has access to a bank account. It promotes financial inclusion by providing banking services to the unbanked population, especially in rural and remote areas.

Basic Financial Services: The scheme offers a range of basic financial services such as a savings account, deposit account, remittance, credit, insurance, and pension. These services empower individuals with access to formal financial systems.

Overdraft Facility: Under PMJDY, eligible account holders can avail overdraft facilities, providing them with access to credit for meeting immediate financial needs.

Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): PMJDY facilitates direct benefit transfers (DBT) of various government subsidies and welfare payments directly into the bank accounts of beneficiaries, reducing leakages and improving transparency.

Financial Literacy and Education: The scheme aims to promote financial literacy and awareness among the rural and urban poor, empowering them with the knowledge to make informed financial decisions and utilize banking services effectively.

Promotion of Electronic Payments: PMJDY encourages the use of digital payment methods such as debit cards, mobile banking, and electronic fund transfers, fostering a cashless economy and reducing reliance on cash transactions.
